# Methodology

## From Homophily-First to Heterophily-Aware Explanations

Standard GNNExplainer optimizes a prediction-fidelity objective with sparsity/entropy regularization over an edge mask $m \in [0,1]^{|E|}$:

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\text{base}} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{pred}} + \lambda_1 \|m\|_1 + \lambda_2 \mathcal{H}(m)
$$

where $\mathcal{L}_{\text{pred}}$ enforces fidelity between the original prediction and masked-graph prediction, while $\|m\|_1$ and $\mathcal{H}(m)$ encourage compact, stable explanations.

In heterophilic graphs, explanatory edges are often dissimilar in feature space. To encode this behavior, we add a feature-distance regularizer based on cosine similarity between neighboring node features:

$$
\mathrm{sim}_{ij} = \cos(\mathbf{x}_i, \mathbf{x}_j) \in [-1,1], \quad (i,j) \in E
$$

$$
\mathcal{R}_{\text{hetero}} = \frac{1}{|E|} \sum_{(i,j) \in E} m_{ij} \cdot \mathrm{sim}_{ij}
$$

The final objective is:

$$
\mathcal{L}_{\text{hetero}} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{base}} + \alpha \cdot \mathcal{R}_{\text{hetero}}
$$

with $\alpha = \texttt{heterophily\_weight} \in [0,1]$ tuned by sweep. This term penalizes selecting highly similar edges (positive cosine) and relatively rewards selecting dissimilar/opposite edges (negative cosine), producing explanations aligned with disassortative mixing.

Normalization by $|E|$ prevents the regularizer from growing solely due to larger candidate subgraphs, improving stability across datasets.

# Results Summary

Across the homophily sweep, baseline topology-first explanations are expected to degrade as homophily decreases, while the heterophily-aware objective should preserve or improve Fidelity+ by selecting dissimilar but task-relevant edges.

A recurring behavior at intermediate homophily (for example around $h=0.5$) is mixed performance: the graph contains both assortative and disassortative neighborhoods, so topology-only explainers can still recover part of the signal, reducing the relative gain margin of the heterophily-aware variant. In very low homophily settings, the proposed regularizer typically has larger impact because dissimilar connections dominate the predictive mechanism.

Interpretation guidance for reporting:

- Fidelity+: Higher is better and indicates recovered supporting structure.
- Fidelity-: Higher is better and indicates the identified edges are truly necessary (removing them hurts prediction more).
- Sparsity: Higher means more compact explanations (larger fraction of edges removed).

For publication tables, report mean +/- standard deviation over 50 evaluated nodes per dataset and include the gap:

$$
\Delta \text{Fidelity+} = \text{Fidelity+}_{\text{hetero}} - \text{Fidelity+}_{\text{baseline}}
$$

The best `heterophily_weight` should be selected by maximizing average $\Delta \text{Fidelity+}$ over all homophily settings.
